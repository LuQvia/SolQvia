Create a Daily Cash Count and Reconciliation Process

Track opening cash, cash sales, movements, physical count, and variance so differences are investigated the same day.

Published: 2026-07-22Last updated: 2026-07-22
In this guide
  1. Basic reconciliation
  2. Closing-sheet fields
  3. Closing workflow
  4. Controls

Basic reconciliation

Expected cash equals opening cash plus cash sales and other receipts, less refunds, expenses, and bank deposits. The difference from the physical count is the cash variance.

Keep card payments and discounts separate from physical cash.

Closing-sheet fields

  • Business date, location, and preparer.
  • Opening cash, cash sales, and other receipts.
  • Cash refunds, expenses, and deposits.
  • Expected balance, physical balance, and variance.
  • Reason, recount person, and approver.

Closing workflow

  1. Finalise cash sales from the POS and sales report.
  2. Review non-sales cash movement documents.
  3. Count cash by denomination.
  4. Calculate the difference from expected cash.
  5. Review cancellations, refunds, and change errors when a variance exists.
  6. Have a manager confirm the next opening balance.

Controls

  • Separate the counter and approver where possible.
  • Do not hide differences in a general expense line.
  • Link payments to receipts and reasons.
  • Analyse recurring differences by time and shift.
Key point

Cash differences become harder to trace with time, so investigate them on the same day.
