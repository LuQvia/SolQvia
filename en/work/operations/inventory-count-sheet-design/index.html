Design an Inventory Count Sheet for Physical, Book, and Variance Quantities

Track item IDs, locations, units, physical counts, book quantities, variance value, and reasons.

Published: 2026-07-22Last updated: 2026-07-22
In this guide
  1. Purpose of inventory counts
  2. Required columns
  3. Count workflow
  4. Reduce future variance

Purpose of inventory counts

Inventory counting compares physical stock with recorded stock to find missing movements, disposal, damage, theft, and unit errors.

Separate counting from variance approval when possible.

Required columns

  • Count date, location, item ID, and item name.
  • Unit, book quantity, and physical quantity.
  • Quantity variance, unit cost, and value variance.
  • Reason, reviewer, and adjustment date.

Count workflow

  1. Set a cutoff when movements stop.
  2. Assign counting areas.
  3. For a blind count, hide book quantities from counters.
  4. Have a second person recount material differences.
  5. After approval, adjust records and classify the reason.

Reduce future variance

  • Standardise item codes and units.
  • Define when receipts, usage, and disposal are recorded.
  • Cycle-count high-value and fast-moving items more often.
  • Review variance reasons monthly.
Key point

Preserve the variance and adjustment history instead of simply replacing the original number.
