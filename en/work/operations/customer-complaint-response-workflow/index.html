Standardise Customer Complaint Handling from Intake to Prevention

Create one workflow for listening, fact-finding, approval, response records, and corrective action.

Published: 2026-07-22Last updated: 2026-07-22
In this guide
  1. Separate feelings, requests, and facts
  2. Standard workflow
  3. Record fields
  4. Avoid

Separate feelings, requests, and facts

At intake, acknowledge the customer while separately recording date, service, staff, symptoms or loss, and requested outcome.

Do not promise a refund or accept liability before the relevant facts and authority are confirmed. State when and how you will respond.

Standard workflow

  1. Listen without interrupting and confirm the key issue.
  2. Assess any immediate health or safety risk.
  3. Check internal records and speak with involved staff.
  4. Approve a response within the correct authority level.
  5. Explain the outcome and next step to the customer.
  6. Record the cause and preventive action.

Record fields

  • Received time, receiver, and customer identifier.
  • Incident, requested outcome, and urgency.
  • Verified facts and supporting evidence.
  • Response, approver, and contact history.
  • Cause category, corrective action, and completion date.

Avoid

  • Concluding liability before fact-finding.
  • Making exceptional promises without authority.
  • Recording only emotional wording and no objective details.
  • Closing the case without root-cause review.
Key point

Escalate legal, medical, or specialist decisions separately from ordinary customer communication.
