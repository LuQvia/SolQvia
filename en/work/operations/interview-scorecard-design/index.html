Create an Interview Scorecard That Reduces Interviewer Variance

Define job-related criteria before interviews and record evidence, examples, scores, and concerns consistently.

Published: 2026-07-22Last updated: 2026-07-22
In this guide
  1. Reduce impression-based decisions
  2. Example competencies
  3. Scoring workflow
  4. Scorecard fields

Reduce impression-based decisions

If the final discussion is based only on whether someone seemed impressive, interviewer preference and question differences can dominate the result.

Define job requirements before interviewing and use common assessment areas for every candidate.

Example competencies

  • Role understanding and learning ability.
  • Customer and team communication.
  • Response to problems and uncertainty.
  • Accuracy and deadline management.
  • Role-specific technical capability.

Scoring workflow

  1. Define observable anchors for scores 1, 3, and 5.
  2. Prepare consistent primary and follow-up questions.
  3. Record facts and specific examples from the answer.
  4. Score independently before group discussion.
  5. Discuss only large scoring differences and their evidence.

Scorecard fields

  • Competency, weight, and scoring anchors.
  • Question, answer summary, and evidence.
  • Score, confidence, and concern.
  • Overall recommendation and follow-up questions.
Key point

Keep the evidence that supports each score, not only the score itself.
