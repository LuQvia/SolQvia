A Simple Cash-Control Sheet for Small Stores

Reconcile book cash and physical cash every day with clear fields for inflows, outflows, differences, and review.

Published: 2026-07-22Last updated: 2026-07-22
In this guide
  1. Purpose of a cash-control sheet
  2. Core fields
  3. Calculation
  4. Operating controls

Purpose of a cash-control sheet

A cash sheet should do more than record sales. Its primary control is to reconcile book cash with the physical count and identify differences promptly.

Assign responsibility by store and day, and retain correction history. Accounting record requirements and retention periods depend on jurisdiction and business circumstances, so confirm them with an appropriate professional.

Core fields

  • Date, location, and preparer
  • Opening cash balance
  • Cash sales
  • Other cash receipts
  • Expenses, refunds, and other cash payments
  • Calculated closing balance
  • Physical cash count
  • Difference, explanation, and reviewer

Calculation

When the difference is not zero, review the day's receipts, change fund, refunds, reimbursements, and missing entries instead of carrying the unexplained variance forward.

Book balance = Opening balance + Cash sales + Other receipts - Cash payments
Difference = Physical cash - Book balance

Operating controls

  • Separate preparation and review where practical.
  • Retain the prior value and record the reason and time for corrections.
  • For expenses without a receipt, document purpose, counterparty, amount, and approval.
  • At month-end, reconcile sales reports, payment-processor deposits, and bank deposits.
Key point

Repeated cash differences usually indicate a process issue in change preparation, refunds, or closing procedures—not only individual carelessness.

This article provides general information. Check official sources for current specifications, prices, rules, and opening hours.
