Customer Complaint and Incident Log

Record time, facts, customer request, response, evidence, owner, and prevention for each case.

Published: 2026-07-22Last updated: 2026-07-22
In this guide
  1. Purpose and operational use
  2. Fields and checks to include
  3. Operating workflow
  4. What reviewers should verify
  5. Ongoing operating rules

Purpose and operational use

Record time, facts, customer request, response, evidence, owner, and prevention for each case.

The key is separating opinion from verified fact and minimizing personal data. For legal requirements and filing deadlines, verify current official guidance and obtain professional advice where appropriate.

Fields and checks to include

  • Time, location, and parties
  • Verified facts and unconfirmed information
  • Customer request and first response
  • Owner, deadline, and prevention
Key point

Prioritize ownership, deadlines, evidence, and approval state over adding more fields.

Operating workflow

  1. Confirm business date, location, owner, and scope.
  2. Record facts and quantities at the point of work and separate exceptions.
  3. Have a reviewer compare evidence with the operating standard.
  4. Assign corrective action, owner, deadline, and recheck date.

What reviewers should verify

  • Can the procedure be performed during real operations?
  • Are stop and escalation conditions explicit?
  • Is there a backup when the owner is absent?
  • Are definitions and records consistent across locations?

Ongoing operating rules

  • Assign an owner and backup
  • Put close dates or review cycles on the calendar
  • Retain exceptions with reason and approval
  • Review fields and permissions monthly or quarterly
  • Check changes to laws, schemes, and official forms
Key point

This article explains general operating design. Verify tax, labor, and legal decisions with current official guidance and qualified advisers.

Frequently asked questions

Does a small business need Customer Complaint and Incident Log?

Yes when the process repeats or changes hands. A lightweight register that fixes separating opinion from verified fact and minimizing personal data can prevent omissions during busy periods.

Do we need dedicated software from the beginning?

No. Start with CSV, a spreadsheet, or a shared document to stabilize fields and approvals. Consider specialist software when volume, integrations, or access control grows.

How often should the process be reviewed?

Review daily controls daily, close activities monthly, and policies, permissions, and forms at least quarterly or whenever requirements change. Verify legal deadlines with current official guidance.
