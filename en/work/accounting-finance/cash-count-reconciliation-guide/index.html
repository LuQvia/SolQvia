Cash Count and Reconciliation: Resolving Variances Before the Next Day

Count till, safe, petty cash, and float balances, then document and approve any variance.

Purpose and control scope

Count till, safe, petty cash, and float balances, then document and approve any variance.

The key is separating the counter and reviewer and never inventing a reason for a variance. For legal requirements and filing deadlines, verify current official guidance and obtain professional advice where appropriate.

Fields and checks to include

  • Cash categories and locations
  • Opening and closing balances
  • Variance amount and reason category
  • Recount, approval, and journal entry
Key point

Prioritize ownership, deadlines, evidence, and approval state over adding more fields.

Operating workflow

  1. Set the period and accountable owner.
  2. Collect evidence and system data using the same cutoff.
  3. List differences, unprocessed items, and exceptions, then verify evidence.
  4. Post approved changes and retain evidence plus carry-forward actions.

What reviewers should verify

  • Can every total be traced to source evidence?
  • Are preparer and approver appropriately separated?
  • Does every open item have an owner and deadline?
  • Are corrections, voids, and recalculations retained?

Ongoing operating rules

  • Assign an owner and backup
  • Put close dates or review cycles on the calendar
  • Retain exceptions with reason and approval
  • Review fields and permissions monthly or quarterly
  • Check changes to laws, schemes, and official forms
Key point

This article explains general operating design. Verify tax, labor, and legal decisions with current official guidance and qualified advisers.

Frequently asked questions

Does a small business need Cash Count and Reconciliation: Resolving Variances Before the Next Day?

Yes when the process repeats or changes hands. A lightweight register that fixes separating the counter and reviewer and never inventing a reason for a variance can prevent omissions during busy periods.

Do we need dedicated software from the beginning?

No. Start with CSV, a spreadsheet, or a shared document to stabilize fields and approvals. Consider specialist software when volume, integrations, or access control grows.

How often should the process be reviewed?

Review daily controls daily, close activities monthly, and policies, permissions, and forms at least quarterly or whenever requirements change. Verify legal deadlines with current official guidance.
