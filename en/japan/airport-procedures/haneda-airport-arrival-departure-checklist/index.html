Haneda Airport International Arrival and Departure Checklist

Plan Haneda Terminal 2 and 3 arrival, immigration, customs, domestic connection, and departure.

Decision summary

Plan Haneda Terminal 2 and 3 arrival, immigration, customs, domestic connection, and departure.

The central decision is checking the operating terminal on the day because international flights use different terminals. Entry, customs, quarantine, and aviation-security conditions vary by nationality, passport, item, airport, and effective date, so check official information immediately before travel.

Fields for the checklist

  • T2 or T3 flight check
  • Arrival and customs
  • Domestic connection and transfer
  • Departure and Face Express eligibility
Key point

Do not rely only on verbal guidance; save official pages, declaration screens, permits, and booking records.

Preparation and procedure workflow

  1. Confirm airline, flight, and terminal
  2. Keep passport, code, and documents ready
  3. Follow the sequence for inspection, baggage, customs, and security
  4. Allow time for transfer and transport deadlines

Final pre-departure review

  • Terminal, counter, and deadline
  • Passport, boarding pass, and code
  • Baggage, dangerous goods, liquids, and batteries
  • Transfer, transport, and buffer time

Prioritize accurate declarations and current rules

  • Match names, passport numbers, and dates across documents
  • Ask staff rather than concealing an uncertain item
  • Keep critical screens available offline
  • Allow enough time for transfer, quarantine, and customs
  • Match the effective date to the date of use
Key point

This guide supports general travel preparation. Current guidance from MOFA, Immigration Services Agency, Japan Customs, quarantine authorities, airports, and airlines takes priority for final decisions.

Frequently asked questions

What should I check first for Haneda Airport International Arrival and Departure Checklist?

Start by checking the operating terminal on the day because international flights use different terminals. Conditions vary by nationality, passport, flight, airport, item, and effective date, so save current official guidance before departure.

Are procedures identical at every airport?

The core sequence is similar, but terminals, kiosks, gates, transfer routes, and hours vary by airport and flight.

How should I save my preparation records?

Keep passport, visa, booking, code, declaration, permit, receipt, and official-page records both on your device and offline.

This article provides general information. Check official sources for current specifications, prices, rules, and opening hours.
