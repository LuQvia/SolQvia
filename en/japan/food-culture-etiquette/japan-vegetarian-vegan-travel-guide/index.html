Vegetarian and Vegan Travel Guide to Japan

Prepare for stocks, seasonings, reservations, and clear dietary explanation cards.

Published: 2026-07-22Last updated: 2026-07-22
In this guide
  1. Why visitors are often unsure
  2. Key points to remember
  3. What to do on site
  4. Allow for local variation
  5. Pre-departure check

Why visitors are often unsure

Japanese dishes may contain fish stock or seasonings that are not visible. Instead of saying only “no meat,” specify whether fish, stock, eggs, dairy, and other ingredients must also be excluded.

SolQvia focuses on a decision process that remains useful when prices, schedules, and local operating conditions change.

Key points to remember

  • Carry a Japanese dietary-requirement card
  • Discuss set menus at the time of reservation
  • Check individual ingredients outside specialist restaurants
  • Consider accommodation with a kitchen in regional areas

What to do on site

  1. Read entrance signs, menus, and posted instructions.
  2. Ask about unclear conditions before using the service.
  3. Follow staff guidance and observe the local flow.
  4. Choose another venue when requirements cannot be confirmed.

Allow for local variation

Practices differ by venue, religious tradition, region, and operator. Local signs and staff instructions take priority over general etiquette guides.

Pre-departure check

  • Check the final update date on official sources
  • Save reservation numbers, addresses, and contacts outside one device
  • Keep room to remove one activity
  • Prioritize safety and health when deciding to cancel
Key point

Prices, schedules, operating conditions, opening days, events, and transport can change. Confirm official information shortly before use.

This article provides general information. Check official sources for current specifications, prices, rules, and opening hours.
