How to Rent and Drive a Car in Japan

Prepare licences, left-side driving, ETC, fuel, parking, and return conditions.

Published: 2026-07-22Last updated: 2026-07-22
In this guide
  1. Decisions to make first
  2. What to compare
  3. Preparation sequence
  4. Items requiring a final check
  5. Pre-departure check

Decisions to make first

Cars are useful in regional Japan, Hokkaido, and Okinawa, but drivers must confirm valid licence documents and local rules. In major cities, compare parking and congestion with public transport.

SolQvia focuses on a decision process that remains useful when prices, schedules, and local operating conditions change.

What to compare

  • Confirm valid international driving documents before travel
  • Check ETC card rental conditions
  • Review refuelling and full-tank return rules
  • Confirm winter tyres and road closures

Preparation sequence

  1. List device, luggage, group, and route requirements.
  2. Compare at least two options by total cost and total time.
  3. Save bookings and required documents for offline access.
  4. Recheck official conditions shortly before departure.

Items requiring a final check

Transport, entry, tax, and service conditions change. Do not rely only on old articles or reviews; confirm the latest operator or government information before use.

Pre-departure check

  • Check the final update date on official sources
  • Save reservation numbers, addresses, and contacts outside one device
  • Keep room to remove one activity
  • Prioritize safety and health when deciding to cancel
Key point

Prices, schedules, operating conditions, opening days, events, and transport can change. Confirm official information shortly before use.

This article provides general information. Check official sources for current specifications, prices, rules, and opening hours.
