Hiroshima Peace Memorial Park Visitor Guide

Visit the Atomic Bomb Dome, Peace Memorial Museum, and memorials with time and respect.

Published: 2026-07-22Last updated: 2026-07-22
In this guide
  1. Who this destination suits
  2. Main highlights
  3. Access and suggested approach
  4. Check before visiting

Who this destination suits

Hiroshima Peace Memorial Park is not simply a photo stop; it is a place to learn about the bombing and the city’s commitment to peace. Visit the Atomic Bomb Dome, memorials, and museum without rushing.

For a first visit, connect the main sights in one logical direction and reserve transport and rest time before adding optional stops.

Main highlights

  • The Atomic Bomb Dome
  • The Flame of Peace and Cenotaph
  • Hiroshima Peace Memorial Museum
  • The riverside park and its memorials

Access and suggested approach

Streetcars and buses connect Hiroshima Station with the Atomic Bomb Dome area. The park can also be reached on foot from central Hiroshima or Hiroshima Castle.

  1. Confirm the arrival point and return transport first.
  2. Choose one non-negotiable highlight.
  3. Connect two or three nearby stops in the same direction.
  4. Leave buffer time for food, rest, and delays.

Check before visiting

  • Allow substantial time for the museum
  • Be prepared for emotionally difficult exhibits
  • Maintain respectful behavior around memorials
  • Check restrictions and crowds on ceremony dates
Key point

Hours, fees, transport, construction, entry restrictions, and seasonal events can change. Confirm official information shortly before travel.

This article provides general information. Check official sources for current specifications, prices, rules, and opening hours.
