Oze National Park Hiking Guide

Prepare transport, equipment, and conservation practices for hiking Oze wetlands.

Published: 2026-07-22Last updated: 2026-07-22
In this guide
  1. Why visit
  2. Main highlights
  3. Suggested order

Why visit

Oze is a protected landscape of wetlands, ponds, and mountains viewed from boardwalks and trails. Seasonal trailhead transport and substantial distances require hiking preparation rather than casual park planning.

Keep the route focused and confirm the return connection first so that transport does not consume the time intended for the destination.

Main highlights

  • The broad Ozegahara wetland
  • Ponds and plants along boardwalks
  • Views of Mount Shibutsu and Mount Hiuchigatake
  • Overnight routes using mountain huts

Suggested order

  1. Begin with The broad Ozegahara wetland.
  2. Continue in the same direction to Ponds and plants along boardwalks.
  3. If time remains, add Views of Mount Shibutsu and Mount Hiuchigatake or Overnight routes using mountain huts.
  4. Preserve buffer time for food, rest, and the return journey.

Access

Multiple trailheads are reached from Gunma, Fukushima, and Niigata by seasonal buses or shared transport. Confirm how the entry and exit trailheads connect.

Check before visiting

  • Bring waterproof hiking shoes and rain gear
  • Stay on boardwalks to protect vegetation
  • Check the last bus and sunset time
  • Review mountain-hut reservation conditions
Key point

Hours, prices, transport, entry rules, construction, and seasonal events change. Recheck official facility, local-government, and transport sources shortly before departure.

Frequently asked questions

How much time should I allow?

Even a short wetland return requires a full day; traverses and hut routes need at least one overnight stay.

Can I visit by public transport?

Multiple trailheads are reached from Gunma, Fukushima, and Niigata by seasonal buses or shared transport. Confirm how the entry and exit trailheads connect.

What should I verify before departure?

Check current hours, transport schedules, weather, and entry restrictions. For mountain, coastal, and onsen areas, also review service suspensions and road closures.

This article provides general information. Check official sources for current specifications, prices, rules, and opening hours.
