What to Do When an IC Card Gate Does Not Open

Resolve IC gate errors caused by low balance, incomplete trip records, poor taps, or area boundaries.

Published: 2026-07-22Last updated: 2026-07-22
In this guide
  1. Decision summary
  2. Fields for comparison and booking
  3. Preparation and use workflow
  4. Final pre-use review
  5. Prioritize official conditions and safety

Decision summary

Resolve IC gate errors caused by low balance, incomplete trip records, poor taps, or area boundaries.

The central decision is not repeatedly tapping and asking station staff to inspect the trip record. Public transport, booking, fare, payment, and disruption conditions vary by region, operator, and date, so check current official information immediately before use.

Fields for comparison and booking

  • Low balance
  • Incomplete entry or exit record
  • Crossing IC areas
  • Staff and fare-adjustment handling
Key point

Save booking screens, ticket or QR, and station, stop, port, or return-port maps for offline access.

Preparation and use workflow

  1. Confirm route and ticket media
  2. Check purchase, issuance, and linkage conditions
  3. Record gates, transfers, seats, and luggage
  4. Ask station staff early for changes or errors

Final pre-use review

  • Route and operator
  • Ticket, IC, or QR type
  • Transfer, seat, and luggage
  • Change, refund, and outage

Prioritize official conditions and safety

  • Use the operator same-day status as the source of truth
  • Follow current signs at gates, stops, ports, and return stations
  • Contact staff or support rather than improvising changes or error handling
  • Keep alternatives for last services, weather, and crowding
  • Protect personal, payment, and reservation data
Key point

This guide supports general travel preparation. Current operator, police, MLIT, and booking-service conditions take priority.

Frequently asked questions

What should I check first for What to Do When an IC Card Gate Does Not Open?

Start by not repeatedly tapping and asking station staff to inspect the trip record. Conditions vary by date, area, operator, and booking media, so save current official screens before use.

Can one IC card cover every trip?

No. Limited-express seats, unsupported sections, and journeys across IC areas may require separate tickets or adjustment.

What records should I keep?

Keep reservation number, ticket or QR, stop, port, or station map, fare terms, vehicle or service number, disruption notices, and receipts.
