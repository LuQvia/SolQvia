ANA International Flight Disruption Guide

Review ANA international change, refund, eligible rerouting, and certificates for weather or airline-caused disruptions.

Decision summary

Review ANA international change, refund, eligible rerouting, and certificates for weather or airline-caused disruptions.

The central decision is checking ticket stock and operating carrier. Policy terms, conditions of carriage, international conventions, and passenger rules differ by scope and date, so confirm the official rules for the incident and itinerary.

Fields for the review table

  • Ticketing airline and stock
  • ANA-operated and codeshare flights
  • Tax and ancillary handling
  • Certificate and contact deadline
Key point

Record carrier support, seller refunds, insurance, and card coverage separately and do not claim the same loss twice.

Incident response workflow

  1. Confirm operator, flight, seller, and cause
  2. Save change, refund, and alternative options
  3. Check approval for meals, hotel, and transport
  4. Keep certificates, receipts, and notices for claims

Pre-report and claim review

  • Operator, ticketing carrier, and seller
  • Cause and departure or arrival delay
  • Refund, rerouting, and ancillaries
  • Hotel, meals, transport, and certificate

Prioritize deadlines, evidence, and safety

  • Prioritize safety and treatment; do not wait for insurance approval in an emergency
  • Check report references and documents before leaving the airport or hospital
  • Save receipts, photos, notices, and call history chronologically
  • Keep official pages, policy terms, and certificates, not only verbal advice
  • Track claim and follow-up deadlines in relevant time zones
Key point

This guide supports general preparation and incident response. Current policy wording, conditions of carriage, carrier, medical provider, regulator, and qualified professional guidance take priority for claims, legal rights, and medical decisions.

Frequently asked questions

What should I check first for ANA International Flight Disruption Guide?

Start by checking ticket stock and operating carrier. Coverage, carrier support, and deadlines vary by policy, route, and cause, so save the official terms in force on the incident date.

Can I claim from both the airline and insurer?

You generally cannot recover the same loss twice. Declare payments from the carrier, seller, card insurance, and standalone policy, then claim only eligible uninsured loss.

What evidence should I always save?

Save booking, boarding pass, certificate, report reference, medical record, receipt, photos, refund amount, agent name, contact time, and policy terms.
