Nara Park and Todaiji Travel Guide

Walk through Nara Park, Todaiji, and nearby heritage sites while interacting with deer responsibly.

Published: 2026-07-22Last updated: 2026-07-22
In this guide
  1. Who this destination suits
  2. Main highlights
  3. Access and suggested approach
  4. Check before visiting

Who this destination suits

Nara Park places Todaiji, Kasuga Taisha, Kofukuji, and other heritage sites within a broad park where deer roam. Allow several hours and balance animal safety with temple opening times.

For a first visit, connect the main sights in one logical direction and reserve transport and rest time before adding optional stops.

Main highlights

  • Todaiji Great Buddha Hall
  • The deer of Nara Park
  • The forest approach to Kasuga Taisha
  • Kofukuji and Sarusawa Pond

Access and suggested approach

The park is within walking distance of Kintetsu Nara Station. From JR Nara Station, walk or use a local bus.

  1. Confirm the arrival point and return transport first.
  2. Choose one non-negotiable highlight.
  3. Connect two or three nearby stops in the same direction.
  4. Leave buffer time for food, rest, and delays.

Check before visiting

  • Feed deer only approved deer crackers
  • Keep maps, bags, and food away from deer
  • Prioritize a few temples rather than overloading the day
  • Carry water and take breaks in hot weather
Key point

Hours, fees, transport, construction, entry restrictions, and seasonal events can change. Confirm official information shortly before travel.
