Kurama to Kibune Hiking Guide

Plan the mountain path between Kurama Temple and Kibune with suitable gear and transport.

Published: 2026-07-22Last updated: 2026-07-22
In this guide
  1. Why visit
  2. Main highlights
  3. Suggested order
  4. Access
  5. Check before visiting

Why visit

Kurama and Kibune lie in the mountains north of Kyoto and can be linked by a forest trail. Stone steps, roots, and slippery conditions after rain require light-hiking preparation.

Keep the route focused and confirm the return connection first so that transport does not consume the time intended for the destination.

Main highlights

  • Kurama Temple approach and main hall
  • The forest trail including the root-covered section
  • Kifune Shrine steps and lanterns
  • Riverside scenery and seasonal dining

Suggested order

  1. Begin with Kurama Temple approach and main hall.
  2. Continue in the same direction to The forest trail including the root-covered section.
  3. If time remains, add Kifune Shrine steps and lanterns or Riverside scenery and seasonal dining.
  4. Preserve buffer time for food, rest, and the return journey.

Access

A common route starts at Eizan Railway Kurama Station and exits via bus or Kibuneguchi Station on the Kibune side. Check current service.

Check before visiting

  • Bring shoes with grip and water
  • Check trail access after rain or snow
  • Finish the mountain section before dark
  • Review reservation rules for Kibune restaurants
Key point

Hours, prices, transport, entry rules, construction, and seasonal events change. Recheck official facility, local-government, and transport sources shortly before departure.

Frequently asked questions

How much time should I allow?

Allow four to six hours for temples and trail or a full day with dining and rest.

Can I visit by public transport?

A common route starts at Eizan Railway Kurama Station and exits via bus or Kibuneguchi Station on the Kibune side. Check current service.

What should I verify before departure?

Check current hours, transport schedules, weather, and entry restrictions. For mountain, coastal, and onsen areas, also review service suspensions and road closures.
