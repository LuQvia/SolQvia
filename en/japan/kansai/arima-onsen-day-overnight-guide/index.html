Arima Onsen Day-Trip and Overnight Guide

Plan Arima gold and silver spring baths, town walks, and an overnight stay from Kobe or Osaka.

Published: 2026-07-22Last updated: 2026-07-22
In this guide
  1. Why visit
  2. Main highlights
  3. Suggested order
  4. Access
  5. Check before visiting

Why visit

Arima is a historic mountain onsen within Kobe, known for gold and silver spring waters, narrow slopes, and temples. Day visitors need bathing reception times; overnight guests should check ryokan transfers.

Keep the route focused and confirm the return connection first so that transport does not consume the time intended for the destination.

Main highlights

  • Public baths using gold and silver spring waters
  • The sloped onsen-town streets
  • Temples and spring sources
  • Ryokan bathing and dining

Suggested order

  1. Begin with Public baths using gold and silver spring waters.
  2. Continue in the same direction to The sloped onsen-town streets.
  3. If time remains, add Temples and spring sources or Ryokan bathing and dining.
  4. Preserve buffer time for food, rest, and the return journey.

Access

Use Kobe Electric Railway, local or highway buses, or connections from Mount Rokko. Check return services and bathing reception deadlines first.

Check before visiting

  • Avoid prolonged bathing according to health and water properties
  • Wear shoes suitable for steep slopes
  • Check day-bath reception times
  • Book accommodation and buses early in peak periods
Key point

Hours, prices, transport, entry rules, construction, and seasonal events change. Recheck official facility, local-government, and transport sources shortly before departure.

Frequently asked questions

How much time should I allow?

Allow half to a full day for a day trip or stay overnight for dinner and multiple baths.

Can I visit by public transport?

Use Kobe Electric Railway, local or highway buses, or connections from Mount Rokko. Check return services and bathing reception deadlines first.

What should I verify before departure?

Check current hours, transport schedules, weather, and entry restrictions. For mountain, coastal, and onsen areas, also review service suspensions and road closures.

This article provides general information. Check official sources for current specifications, prices, rules, and opening hours.
