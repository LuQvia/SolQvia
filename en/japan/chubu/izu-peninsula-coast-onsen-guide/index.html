Izu Peninsula Coast and Onsen Guide

Compare eastern, southern, and western Izu for coastlines, onsen, beaches, and scenic drives.

Published: 2026-07-22Last updated: 2026-07-22
In this guide
  1. Who this destination suits
  2. Main highlights
  3. Access and suggested approach
  4. Check before visiting

Who this destination suits

The Izu Peninsula spreads beaches, onsen, mountains, and rugged coastlines across a large area. Rather than covering everything, choose the rail-accessible east or the scenic southern and western coasts.

For a first visit, connect the main sights in one logical direction and reserve transport and rest time before adding optional stops.

Main highlights

  • Onsen and museums around Atami and Ito
  • Beaches and history around Shimoda
  • Western Izu coastlines and sunsets
  • Inland onsen towns such as Shuzenji

Access and suggested approach

Enter via Atami from Tokyo, then use the Ito Line, Izu Kyuko, buses, or a car. Western Izu relies more heavily on buses or driving.

  1. Confirm the arrival point and return transport first.
  2. Choose one non-negotiable highlight.
  3. Connect two or three nearby stops in the same direction.
  4. Leave buffer time for food, rest, and delays.

Check before visiting

  • Limit the trip to one or two subregions
  • Check transport during typhoons or strong winds
  • Confirm beach seasons and safety notices
  • Allow for congestion on coastal roads
Key point

Hours, fees, transport, construction, entry restrictions, and seasonal events can change. Confirm official information shortly before travel.

This article provides general information. Check official sources for current specifications, prices, rules, and opening hours.
