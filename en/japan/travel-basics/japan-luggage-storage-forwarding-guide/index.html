How to Store or Forward Luggage While Travelling in Japan

Choose between coin lockers, staffed storage, airport delivery, and hotel-to-hotel forwarding.

Published: 2026-07-22Last updated: 2026-07-22
In this guide
  1. Three common options
  2. Choose by situation
  3. Before forwarding a bag
  4. Items that may not be accepted

Three common options

For a few hours, use station or attraction lockers. Staffed counters can help with different sizes, while delivery services are useful when moving to the next hotel.

Large luggage, busy periods, and major stations can create capacity problems, so check alternatives and operating hours.

Choose by situation

  • Coin locker: short storage for several hours or the day.
  • Staffed storage: size assistance or a different storage period.
  • Airport delivery: start sightseeing without taking bags into the city.
  • Hotel-to-hotel delivery: simplify intercity travel and local-station transfers.

Before forwarding a bag

  1. Confirm that the next accommodation accepts delivered luggage.
  2. Check the cutoff time for the requested arrival date.
  3. Write the guest name, stay date, and reservation name accurately.
  4. Keep medicine, valuables, chargers, and essential clothing with you.
  5. Retain the receipt and tracking number.

Items that may not be accepted

  • Cash, passports, and high-value items.
  • Fragile items, liquids, and fresh food.
  • Dangerous goods or temperature-controlled items.
  • Baggage above the provider’s size or weight limits.
Key point

Prices, storage periods, limits, and same-day coverage vary by provider and location. Check official terms.

This article provides general information. Check official sources for current specifications, prices, rules, and opening hours.
