What to Do If an Earthquake Occurs During a Trip to Japan

Prepare for earthquakes in hotels, stations, and coastal areas with safety actions, official information, and emergency contacts.

Published: 2026-07-22Last updated: 2026-07-22
In this guide
  1. During the shaking
  2. After the shaking
  3. Prepare before travel
  4. Official information and contacts

During the shaking

Protect your head and body from falling objects, glass, and furniture. In buildings and stations, do not rush toward exits or tracks; follow staff and public announcements.

Do not use elevators. If trapped, use the emergency button or communication system.

After the shaking

  1. Check for injury and fire danger.
  2. Confirm evacuation routes and assembly points.
  3. Move to higher ground after strong coastal shaking or a tsunami warning.
  4. Check official railway, airline, and road information.
  5. Avoid unnecessary calls and send a short status message to family.

Prepare before travel

  • Power bank and charging cable.
  • Water, regular medicine, and simple food.
  • Passport copy and travel-insurance details.
  • Accommodation, embassy, and emergency contacts.
  • Offline maps and a translation option.

Official information and contacts

Japan uses 110 for urgent police assistance and 119 for fire or ambulance. JNTO provides emergency guidance and a Japan Visitor Hotline. Save the official page shortly before travel because contact details and language support can change.

Key point

In a coastal tsunami risk area, do not return for luggage. Follow warnings and evacuation signs to higher ground.

This article provides general information. Check official sources for current specifications, prices, rules, and opening hours.
