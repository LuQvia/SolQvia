Beppu Onsen and Hells Guide

Understand Beppu hot-spring districts, the Hells, bathing facilities, and local transport.

Published: 2026-07-22Last updated: 2026-07-22
In this guide
  1. Who this destination suits
  2. Main highlights
  3. Access and suggested approach
  4. Check before visiting

Who this destination suits

In Beppu, the high-temperature “Hells” are for viewing, while separate facilities are used for bathing. Because the city has several onsen districts, avoid overloading one area with sightseeing, bathing, and accommodation plans.

For a first visit, connect the main sights in one logical direction and reserve transport and rest time before adding optional stops.

Main highlights

  • The varied colors and steam of the Hells
  • Public baths and onsen accommodation
  • Steam-cooked jigoku-mushi food
  • Views between Beppu Bay and the mountains

Access and suggested approach

Reach Beppu by rail or bus from Oita Airport, Hakata, and other cities. Local buses serve the Hells area.

  1. Confirm the arrival point and return transport first.
  2. Choose one non-negotiable highlight.
  3. Connect two or three nearby stops in the same direction.
  4. Leave buffer time for food, rest, and delays.

Check before visiting

  • Do not enter the sightseeing Hells
  • Review onsen etiquette before bathing
  • Bring a towel and water
  • Do not force yourself into water that feels too hot
Key point

Hours, fees, transport, construction, entry restrictions, and seasonal events can change. Confirm official information shortly before travel.

This article provides general information. Check official sources for current specifications, prices, rules, and opening hours.
