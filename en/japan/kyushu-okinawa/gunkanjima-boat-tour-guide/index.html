Gunkanjima Boat Tour Guide

Plan a Gunkanjima landing tour around operators, sea conditions, landing rules, and Nagasaki sightseeing.

Published: 2026-07-22Last updated: 2026-07-22
In this guide
  1. Why visit
  2. Main highlights
  3. Suggested order
  4. Access

Why visit

Gunkanjima, or Hashima, is a former coal-mining island accessible only through approved tours and designated paths. Even after departure, sea conditions may prevent landing, so book with an understanding that it may remain a cruise-only view.

Keep the route focused and confirm the return connection, weather alternative, and primary purpose before departure so transport does not consume the destination time.

Main highlights

  • Exterior views of the island from the boat
  • Designated landing route when permitted
  • Coal-mining and industrial-heritage interpretation
  • Nagasaki Port and related museums

Suggested order

  1. Begin with Exterior views of the island from the boat as the main objective.
  2. Continue in the same direction to Designated landing route when permitted.
  3. If time and energy remain, add Coal-mining and industrial-heritage interpretation or Nagasaki Port and related museums.
  4. Preserve buffer time for meals, rest, luggage, and the return journey.

Access

Use the designated pier of the selected operator around Nagasaki Port. Boarding point, check-in, identification, and cancellation rules differ by company.

Check before visiting

  • Use an approved operator and review booking rules
  • Understand landing criteria and cancellation policies
  • Prepare for motion sickness
  • Remain on designated paths and follow safety instructions
Key point

Hours, prices, transport, entry rules, construction, natural conditions, and seasonal events change. Recheck official facility, local-government, and transport sources shortly before departure.

Frequently asked questions

How much time should I allow?

The tour takes about half a day; allow a full day with museums and related Nagasaki heritage.

Can I visit by public transport?

Use the designated pier of the selected operator around Nagasaki Port. Boarding point, check-in, identification, and cancellation rules differ by company.

What should I verify before departure?

Check current hours, transport schedules, weather, and entry restrictions. For mountains, islands, coasts, and onsen areas, also review closures, cancellations, and natural conditions.

This article provides general information. Check official sources for current specifications, prices, rules, and opening hours.
