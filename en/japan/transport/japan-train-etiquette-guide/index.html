Transport in Japan

Basic Train Etiquette for Travellers in Japan

Learn boarding lines, phone etiquette, luggage handling, priority seating, and considerate behaviour on crowded trains.

Published: 2026-07-22Last updated: 2026-07-22
In this guide
  1. Before boarding
  2. On the train
  3. Shinkansen and long-distance trains
  4. When unsure

Before boarding

Queue according to platform markings and let passengers leave before boarding. Stay behind safety lines and away from platform doors.

During crowding, avoid stopping at the doorway when space is available further inside.

On the train

  • Set phones to silent and avoid calls.
  • Keep conversation, videos, and music quiet.
  • Hold backpacks in front or use the luggage rack.
  • Give priority seating to passengers who need it.
  • Consider the train type and surrounding passengers before eating or drinking.

Shinkansen and long-distance trains

Eating and drinking are generally accepted on Shinkansen services, but consider smell, noise, and seat reclining. Some routes require or recommend reserved spaces for oversized baggage.

Use vestibules or designated areas for phone calls.

When unsure

  1. Read station and carriage signs.
  2. Move somewhere that does not block passenger flow.
  3. Ask staff rather than forcing a ticket gate when there is a fare or route issue.
  4. Consider waiting for the next train during severe crowding.
Key point

Rules vary by operator and train type. Follow local signage and staff instructions.

This article provides general information. Check official sources for current specifications, prices, rules, and opening hours.
