How to Use an IC Travel Card on Your First Trip to Japan

Understand prepaid cards such as Suica and PASMO, ticket gates, top-ups, buses, and situations requiring separate tickets.

Published: 2026-07-22Last updated: 2026-07-22
In this guide

  2. Basic use
  3. Situations needing extra attention

What an IC travel card does

An IC travel card is a prepaid card used by tapping compatible train gates and bus readers. It can also pay at many participating convenience stores, kiosks, and vending machines.

Regional cards have different names, and many participate in nationwide interoperability. However, not every line, route, or transport operator accepts every card.

Basic use

  1. Check current sales methods and availability at the airport or railway operator.
  2. Add value using an accepted payment method.
  3. Tap the same card when entering and leaving a railway journey.
  4. On buses, follow the displayed instructions for tapping when boarding or leaving.
  5. Use a fare-adjustment machine or ask staff when the balance is insufficient.

Situations needing extra attention

  • Shinkansen and limited-express trips may require a separate ticket or reservation link.
  • Some journeys crossing service areas cannot be completed with an IC card alone.
  • Some ticket machines and top-ups may require cash.
  • Child cards may require age identification.
  • Visitor cards can have different validity and refund conditions from ordinary cards.

Check before travel

  • Which card is sold at your arrival airport or first station.
  • Whether the mobile version supports your phone and payment card.
  • Acceptance on rural railways and highway buses.
  • How you will use the remaining balance before departure.
Key point

Availability and visitor-card conditions can change. Confirm the railway operator’s official information shortly before travel.

This article provides general information. Check official sources for current specifications, prices, rules, and opening hours.
