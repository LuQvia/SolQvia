Bringing Fruit, Vegetables, and Plants into Japan

Handle fruit, vegetables, seeds, seedlings, cut flowers, and plant products under Japan plant-quarantine rules.

Published: 2026-07-22Last updated: 2026-07-22

  1. Decision summary
  2. Fields for the checklist

Decision summary

Handle fruit, vegetables, seeds, seedlings, cut flowers, and plant products under Japan plant-quarantine rules.

The central decision is recognizing that small souvenirs and duty-free purchases may still require quarantine. Entry, customs, quarantine, and aviation-security conditions vary by nationality, passport, item, airport, and effective date, so check official information immediately before travel.

Fields for the checklist

  • Import-prohibited item search
  • Phytosanitary certificate
  • Plant quarantine before customs
  • Soil, pests, and packaging
Key point

Do not rely only on verbal guidance; save official pages, declaration screens, permits, and booking records.

Preparation and procedure workflow

  1. Classify food, medicine, valuables, and unaccompanied baggage
  2. Check customs and quarantine conditions and certificates
  3. Declare accurately through Visit Japan Web or paper
  4. Follow officer instructions and keep records

Final pre-departure review

  • Allowance, duty, prohibition, and restriction
  • Quantity, value, ingredient, and origin
  • Certificate, permit, and declaration
  • Quarantine order and customs inspection

Prioritize accurate declarations and current rules

  • Match names, passport numbers, and dates across documents
  • Ask staff rather than concealing an uncertain item
  • Keep critical screens available offline
  • Allow enough time for transfer, quarantine, and customs
  • Match the effective date to the date of use
Key point

This guide supports general travel preparation. Current guidance from MOFA, Immigration Services Agency, Japan Customs, quarantine authorities, airports, and airlines takes priority for final decisions.

Frequently asked questions

What should I check first for Bringing Fruit, Vegetables, and Plants into Japan?

Start by recognizing that small souvenirs and duty-free purchases may still require quarantine. Conditions vary by nationality, passport, flight, airport, item, and effective date, so save current official guidance before departure.

Should I declare an item if I am unsure?

Yes. Use the red channel or ask customs or quarantine staff and present the item and supporting documents.

How should I save my preparation records?

Keep passport, visa, booking, code, declaration, permit, receipt, and official-page records both on your device and offline.

This article provides general information. Check official sources for current specifications, prices, rules, and opening hours.
