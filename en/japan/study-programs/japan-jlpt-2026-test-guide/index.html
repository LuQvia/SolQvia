Decision summary

Plan 2026 JLPT Guide for N1 to N5 around choosing the level required for the goal and confirming which session is offered locally, with practical steps for applications, residence, cost, work, and daily setup.

The central decision is choosing the level required for the goal and confirming which session is offered locally. Rules vary by nationality, school, program, status, year, and employer, so recheck official information immediately before applying or signing.

The 2026 JLPT is scheduled for July 5 and December 6; some overseas cities offer only one session.

Items to verify

  • Choosing N1 through N5
  • Domestic and overseas sites
  • Application period and test voucher
  • Results and certificate
Key point

Save guidelines, official URLs, application numbers, payment records, submitted documents, deadlines, contacts, and permission details for offline access.

Preparation and application workflow

  1. Confirm each target school's test requirement
  2. Choose subjects, level, site, and date
  3. Save registration, payment, and voucher
  4. Manage score delivery and application deadlines

Final pre-action review

  • Eligible status, school, or program
  • Application, exam, and contract deadlines
  • Cost, payment, and refund
  • Housing, insurance, and emergency contacts

Prioritize official and written conditions

  • Cross-check school and authority information
  • Save written terms rather than relying on verbal explanations
  • Keep actual activities within the residence status
  • Do not depend only on scholarships or part-time income
  • Prepare alternatives for changes, denial, or withdrawal
Key point

This guide supports general planning. Current rules from the school, Immigration Services Agency, embassy or consulate, testing body, and employer take priority.

Confirm the exam, subjects, session, language, and application deadline required by each target school.

What records should I retain?

Keep official URLs, guidelines, application numbers, submitted documents, payment records, deadlines, contacts, contracts, and permission details.
