Decision summary

Plan Starting City Gas Service in Japan around confirming the supplier, gas type, appointment, and appliance compatibility, with practical control of contracts, costs, deadlines, and evidence.

The central decision is confirming the supplier, gas type, appointment, and appliance compatibility. Conditions vary by region, property, provider, residence situation, building facilities, and application timing, so recheck official information and the contract immediately before action.

Items to verify

  • Service area and provider
  • Start, stop, and appointments
  • Rates, payment, and termination
  • Outage and emergency contacts
Key point

Keep contracts, important-matters explanations, quotes, tariffs, equipment photos, application numbers, bills, cancellation and return receipts, and communications.

Operational workflow

  1. Confirm the address and provider
  2. Apply for start date, account name, and payment
  3. Verify equipment and the first bill
  4. Schedule stop service and final settlement

Final pre-action review

  • Contract party and contacts
  • Total and extra costs
  • Start, renewal, and cancellation deadlines
  • Photos, itemized bills, and application receipts

Prioritize official terms and evidence

  • Reconcile advertising with the contract
  • Do not rely only on verbal explanations
  • Read special and cancellation clauses
  • Keep dated photos
  • Use Consumer Hotline 188 or another official channel when needed
Key point

This guide provides general operational organization. Current terms from MLIT, consumer authorities, municipalities, property managers, providers, and the signed contract take priority.

Frequently asked questions

What should I check first for Starting City Gas Service in Japan?

Start by confirming the supplier, gas type, appointment, and appliance compatibility. Save current written information from the contract party, manager, municipality, and provider.

Will the property manager handle every utility procedure?

It varies. Confirm who handles account names, start and stop service, appointments, and final bills for each provider.

What records should I keep?

Keep advertisements, quotes, explanations, leases, special clauses, photos, application numbers, bills, cancellation and return receipts, and communications.
