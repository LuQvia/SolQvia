What to Do if a Major Earthquake Strikes While Driving in Japan

Respond to an earthquake by avoiding sudden braking, stopping left, checking information, and evacuating safely.

Published: 2026-07-22Last updated: 2026-07-22
In this guide
  1. Decision summary
  2. Fields for the driving plan
  3. Preparation and use workflow
  4. Final pre-departure review
  5. Prioritize safety and official conditions

Decision summary

Respond to an earthquake by avoiding sudden braking, stopping left, checking information, and evacuating safely.

The central decision is slowing gradually to the left while preserving emergency access. Driving, toll, road-control, and rental conditions vary by vehicle, region, season, and date, so check current official information before departure.

Fields for the driving plan

  • Avoid sudden control inputs
  • Stop left and switch off
  • Radio and road information
  • Vehicle procedure if evacuating on foot
Key point

Save fees, signs, rental terms, and vehicle condition in photos or PDFs.

Preparation and use workflow

  1. Prevent secondary crashes
  2. Assist injuries and call 119 or 110
  3. Contact road operator, JAF, and rental company
  4. Save photos, location, documents, and expenses

Final pre-departure review

  • Location and direction
  • Injuries and secondary risk
  • 110, 119, and road contact
  • Insurance, office, and evidence

Prioritize safety and official conditions

  • Follow signs, signals, police, and road-operator instructions
  • Use conservative speed, spacing, and rest planning
  • Report crashes, breakdowns, and violations correctly from the scene
  • Obtain rental-office approval for contract changes
  • Stop driving when weather or restrictions make travel unsafe
Key point

This guide supports general travel preparation. Current conditions from police, road operators, rental companies, and insurers take priority.

Frequently asked questions

What should I check first for What to Do if a Major Earthquake Strikes While Driving in Japan?

Start by slowing gradually to the left while preserving emergency access. Conditions vary by date, vehicle, region, weather, and road control, so save current official and booking information.

Should I call police for a minor collision?

Yes. Call 110 from the scene even without injury, then notify the rental company and insurer.

What records should I keep?

Keep licence and passport, rental contract, vehicle photos, signs and prices, toll, fuel and parking receipts, plus police and rental-company contacts.

This article provides general information. Check official sources for current specifications, prices, rules, and opening hours.
