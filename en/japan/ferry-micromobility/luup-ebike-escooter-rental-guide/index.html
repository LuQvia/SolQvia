Using LUUP E-Bikes and E-Scooters

Decision summary

Use LUUP by service city, vehicle type, unlock and per-minute price, app, ports, return, and support.

The central decision is confirming the in-app price and return port before starting. Public transport, booking, fare, payment, and disruption conditions vary by region, operator, and date, so check current official information immediately before use.

As of July 2026, official guidance lists a 50-yen unlock plus 20 yen per minute in Tokyo and Osaka, and 15 yen per minute in other major areas, subject to local variation.

Fields for comparison and booking

  • Cities and vehicles
  • Unlock and per-minute rate
  • QR unlock and destination
  • Return port and support
Key point

Save booking screens, ticket or QR, and station, stop, port, or return-port maps for offline access.

Preparation and use workflow

  1. Confirm area, vehicle, and eligibility
  2. Set price and return port
  3. Inspect the vehicle and follow traffic rules
  4. Record completion, defect, or crash in the app

Final pre-use review

  • Eligibility and vehicle
  • Price and port
  • Rules and equipment
  • Return, defect, and crash

Prioritize official conditions and safety

  • Use the operator same-day status as the source of truth
  • Follow current signs at gates, stops, ports, and return stations
  • Contact staff or support rather than improvising changes or error handling
  • Keep alternatives for last services, weather, and crowding
  • Protect personal, payment, and reservation data
Key point

This guide supports general travel preparation. Current operator, police, MLIT, and booking-service conditions take priority.

Frequently asked questions

What should I check first for Using LUUP E-Bikes and E-Scooters?

Start by confirming the in-app price and return port before starting. Conditions vary by date, area, operator, and booking media, so save current official screens before use.

Is locking the vehicle enough to finish?

Some services require a return-complete confirmation in the app. Contact support for full ports or connectivity issues.

What records should I keep?

Keep reservation number, ticket or QR, stop, port, or station map, fare terms, vehicle or service number, disruption notices, and receipts.
