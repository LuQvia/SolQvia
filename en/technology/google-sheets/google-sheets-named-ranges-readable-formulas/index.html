Make Google Sheets Formulas Easier to Read with Named Ranges

Assign meaningful names to rates, dates, targets, and master ranges instead of relying only on cell coordinates.

Published: 2026-07-22Last updated: 2026-07-22
In this guide
  1. Benefits of named ranges
  2. Formula comparison
  3. Naming rules
  4. Maintenance workflow

Benefits of named ranges

A meaningful name can make a reference such as A1:B100 easier to understand. Named ranges are useful for tax rates, reporting dates, sales data, and master tables.

Too many names can become difficult to maintain, so reserve them for important shared inputs and ranges.

Formula comparison

A name communicates business meaning more clearly than coordinates alone.

=SUM(sales_amount) * tax_rate

Naming rules

  • Use a consistent pattern based on letters, numbers, and underscores.
  • Choose descriptive names such as sales_amount and tax_rate.
  • Distinguish single-value inputs from multi-cell ranges.
  • Review the target after rows, columns, or sheets move.

Maintenance workflow

  1. Place important assumptions on a configuration sheet.
  2. Create the named ranges.
  3. Replace existing references in controlled stages.
  4. Reconcile totals after changing the ranges.
  5. Review the list and remove unused names.
Key point

Named ranges are a form of formula documentation. Share the naming convention with the team.
