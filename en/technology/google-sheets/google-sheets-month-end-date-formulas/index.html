Calculate Month Start and Month End Automatically in Google Sheets

Use DATE, EOMONTH, and EDATE to define reporting periods and closing dates without manual updates.

Published: 2026-07-22Last updated: 2026-07-22
In this guide
  1. Build reporting dates from one month selector
  2. Core formulas
  3. Period-total example

Build reporting dates from one month selector

Monthly reports are easier to maintain when the period start and end are calculated in separate cells and reused in SUMIFS or FILTER conditions.

Use a real date value internally instead of relying on display text such as 2026/07.

Core formulas

  • Month start when B1 contains a date: =EOMONTH(B1,-1)+1
  • Month end: =EOMONTH(B1,0)
  • Next month start: =EOMONTH(B1,0)+1
  • Same day one month later: =EDATE(B1,1)

Period-total example

When B2 is month start and B3 is next month start, the less-than condition works reliably for records containing times.

=SUMIFS($D:$D,$A:$A,">="&$B$2,$A:$A,"<"&$B$3)

Validation checklist

  • Confirm that the date column is not text.
  • Set the spreadsheet time zone to the operating region.
  • Do not assume every month ends on day 31.
  • Avoid depending on 23:59:59 as the final instant.
  • Define fiscal-year versus calendar-year logic.
Key point

An exclusive next-period boundary is usually safer than an inclusive end-of-day condition.
