How to Remove Duplicate Rows Safely in Google Sheets

Define the duplicate key, review candidates, and compare totals before and after deleting rows.

Published: 2026-07-22Last updated: 2026-07-22
In this guide
  1. Define what duplicate means
  2. A safer workflow
  3. Candidate formula
  4. Prevent recurrence

Define what duplicate means

Two people may share a name, and multiple users may share an email address. Before deleting anything, define which fields make two rows the same business record.

A customer ID is ideal. For form responses, a combination such as response ID, timestamp, and email may be more appropriate.

A safer workflow

  1. Duplicate the sheet to create a backup.
  2. Normalize spaces, character width, and letter case in helper columns.
  3. Use COUNTIF or COUNTIFS to identify candidate duplicates.
  4. Review candidates and finalize the deletion rule.
  5. Run Remove duplicates, then compare row counts and financial totals before and after.

Candidate formula

This flags repeated combinations of columns B and C. Use it in a helper column or conditional-formatting rule before deleting rows.

=COUNTIFS($B:$B,B2,$C:$C,C2)>1

Prevent recurrence

  • Assign stable customer, order, or record IDs.
  • Validate duplicate identifiers at entry time.
  • Separate master-record updates from new-history rows.
  • Create a recurring duplicate-candidate report.
Key point

Always create a backup, define the key, and compare counts before treating duplicate removal as complete.
