Generative AI Risk Management: Accuracy, Privacy, Copyright, and Policy

Classify generative AI risks and apply controls before input, during generation, before publication, and during ongoing operation.

Published: 2026-07-22Last updated: 2026-07-22
In this guide
  1. Objective
  2. Inputs to prepare
  3. Core workflow
  4. How to review the output
  5. More advanced use

Objective

Generative AI risk is not a binary question of use or non-use. Controls should match the workflow, input data, publication scope, and impact of error.

The purpose is not to delegate the entire responsibility to AI, but to transform work into a format that is easier for a person to review and decide on.

Inputs to prepare

  • Data terms for the service and plan
  • Confidentiality and rights status of inputs
  • Output destination, audience, and decision impact
  • Reviewers, evidence sources, retention, and deletion period

Core workflow

  1. Classify personal data, confidential information, and copyrighted material before input.
  2. Remove unnecessary information and anonymize where possible.
  3. Review facts, quotations, code, and images with type-specific checks.
  4. Require human approval before publishing or sending.
  5. Record incidents, errors, and terms changes, then update policy.

How to review the output

  • Whether generated content is incorrectly assumed to be unrestricted
  • Whether citations exist and support the claim
  • Whether discriminatory, biased, or inappropriate personal judgments appear
  • Whether users have a channel for reporting errors

More advanced use

  • Classify workflows as prohibited, approval-required, or generally permitted
  • Add dual review and logging to high-impact workflows
  • Review data terms, pricing, and policies for major services periodically
Key point

For repeat use, standardize the input form, approver, storage location, and review date.

Frequently asked questions

How much can be automated?

AI is useful for structuring, drafting, generating options, explaining, and assisting checks. Final approval and exception handling should remain assigned to a person.

How can I improve accuracy?

Specify the objective, audience, input definitions, required output format, prohibited content, and a good example.

What should I do when the output is wrong?

Check the evidence, split the input conditions, and rerun the task. Repeated errors may indicate a source-data or process-definition problem rather than a prompt problem.
