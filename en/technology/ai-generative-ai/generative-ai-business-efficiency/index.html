How to Improve Business Efficiency with Generative AI

Adopt generative AI through workflow selection, risk classification, pilot testing, measurement, and standardization.

Published: 2026-07-22Last updated: 2026-07-22
In this guide
  1. Objective
  2. Inputs to prepare
  3. Core workflow
  4. How to review the output
  5. More advanced use

Objective

Generative AI adoption should not begin as unrestricted organization-wide use. Start with frequent workflows whose inputs and outputs are reviewable and whose failure impact is limited.

The purpose is not to delegate the entire responsibility to AI, but to transform work into a format that is easier for a person to review and decide on.

Inputs to prepare

  • Workflow list, frequency, time, and owners
  • Data confidentiality classification
  • Current quality standards and approvers
  • Baseline time, rework, and error counts

Core workflow

  1. Classify work into creation, organization, retrieval, and decision.
  2. Filter candidates by failure impact and data sensitivity.
  3. Run a small-group pilot for a defined period.
  4. Measure time, quality, review effort, and adoption.
  5. Standardize only workflows with demonstrated value.

How to review the output

  • Whether the before-and-after workflow scope is equivalent
  • Whether review effort exceeds time saved
  • Whether undetected errors move downstream
  • Whether the procedure is understandable to non-users as well

More advanced use

  • Maintain approved prompts and examples by department
  • Turn errors and corrections into internal knowledge
  • Compare usage records with operational KPIs regularly
Key point

For repeat use, standardize the input form, approver, storage location, and review date.

Frequently asked questions

How much can be automated?

AI is useful for structuring, drafting, generating options, explaining, and assisting checks. Final approval and exception handling should remain assigned to a person.

How can I improve accuracy?

Specify the objective, audience, input definitions, required output format, prohibited content, and a good example.

What should I do when the output is wrong?

Check the evidence, split the input conditions, and rerun the task. Repeated errors may indicate a source-data or process-definition problem rather than a prompt problem.
