What Is Microsoft Copilot? Consumer Copilot vs Microsoft 365 Copilot

Understand consumer Copilot, Microsoft 365 Copilot, Microsoft Graph grounding, permissions, and business data protections.

Published: 2026-07-22Last updated: 2026-07-22
In this guide
  1. Overview
  2. Core capabilities
  3. Getting started
  4. Practical use cases
  5. Risks and checks

Overview

Microsoft provides consumer Copilot and Microsoft 365 Copilot for organizational work across Microsoft 365 data and apps. The available data scope differs by account, license, and sign-in context.

For adoption, define the target workflow, human review responsibility, and data rules before focusing on feature volume.

Core capabilities

  • Capabilities are available across Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Outlook, Teams, and other apps
  • Microsoft 365 Copilot can ground responses in permitted Microsoft Graph data
  • It is designed not to access organizational data the user cannot access
  • Privacy terms differ between personal and work or school sign-in contexts
Key point

Feature names and usage limits change. This guide emphasizes durable selection criteria and operating practices.

Getting started

  1. Confirm whether you are signed in with a personal or work/school account.
  2. Review Microsoft 365 sharing and overexposed content.
  3. Confirm the available Copilot features and license.
  4. Test with a small document, email, or spreadsheet workflow.
  5. Review the information and permissions used in the response.

Practical use cases

Treat outputs as drafts, options, structured notes, or comparison support rather than automatically approved final work.

  • assisting drafting and summarization in Word
  • organizing email threads in Outlook
  • reviewing meeting and related content in Teams
  • suggesting formulas or analysis approaches in Excel

Risks and checks

  • Do not assume every product named Copilot has identical data protections
  • Poor Microsoft 365 permissions can expose unnecessary content to authorized search
  • AI output does not replace formal records or approvals
  • Confirm licensing and enabled features with the administrator
Key point

High-impact legal, medical, financial, contractual, and safety decisions require primary sources or qualified professional review.

Frequently asked questions

What should I try first?

Start with a small, reviewable task such as “assisting drafting and summarization in Word” so a person can verify the result.

Can I use it for free?

Free access and trial conditions can change, so check the official pricing page when you sign up.

Can I enter confidential information?

Do not paste personal, customer, or unpublished information without checking the plan’s data terms and your organization’s policy. Anonymize data when possible.

This article provides general information. Check official sources for current specifications, prices, rules, and opening hours.
