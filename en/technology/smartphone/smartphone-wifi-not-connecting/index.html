Phone Not Connecting to Wi-Fi: iPhone and Android Fixes

Troubleshoot missing networks, password errors and “connected but no internet” problems on iPhone and Android.

Data impact: low

These steps normally do not erase stored data. Read any confirmation screen and app-specific notice.

Before you start

Do not begin with a broad reset. Check the following first so you can isolate the problem and avoid unnecessary data loss or reconfiguration.

  • Check whether other devices can use the same network
  • Make sure Airplane Mode is not enabled
  • On public or managed Wi-Fi, look for a sign-in or terms page

Critical diagnosis for this symptom

接続先を変えて原因範囲を確認

Compare the phone on another Wi-Fi network and another device on the same network.

Before changing settings

Confirm the password and authentication requirements before forgetting the network.

When to escalate

Escalate with device, OS, error, and reproduction details when the issue persists after restart, controlled comparison, and update checks, or when physical damage, abnormal heat, account suspension, or carrier provisioning is suspected.

Understand the process

Safe step-by-step procedure

Work from the top and stop as soon as the issue is resolved. Read every confirmation screen before moving to a higher-impact action.

  1. 1
    Turn Wi-Fi off, wait about ten seconds and turn it on again.
  2. 2
    Select the network and re-enter the password, checking capitalization, similar characters and trailing spaces.
  3. 3
    If connected without internet, open a browser and look for a hotel or public-network sign-in page.
  4. 4
    Restart the phone and router; check with the administrator before unplugging managed equipment.
  5. 5
    If only one network fails, forget it and connect again.
  6. 6
    Consider resetting network settings only after simpler steps; saved Wi-Fi and Bluetooth settings may be removed.

Actions to avoid

  • Factory-resetting before identifying whether the phone or router is at fault
  • Signing into sensitive accounts on an untrusted public network
  • Restarting someone else’s router without permission

Prevention and preparation

  • Keep the home network name and password in a secure place
  • Keep the phone OS and router firmware updated
If the issue remains

Record whether the symptom occurs across apps, networks or accessories. Give official support the model, OS version, time, error text and steps already attempted.

Frequently asked questions

What if my screen looks different?

Labels and locations vary by OS, manufacturer, model and region. Use Settings search and check the manufacturer’s model-specific instructions.

What if I am unsure partway through?

Go back and confirm that no action has been committed. Do not proceed through an erase or account-change warning until you understand it.

What if the problem remains?

Test another connection or accessory where relevant, verify backups, then contact the manufacturer or carrier through official support.

This is general operational guidance. Screens and impact vary by OS, model, carrier and device management. Verify official instructions and backups before erasing data, changing accounts or arranging repair.
