eSIM Will Not Activate or Add: QR Code and Transfer Checklist

Resolve eSIM QR-code, pending-activation, and missing-line problems by checking device support, Wi-Fi, one-time codes, transfer rules, and carrier provisioning.

Data impact：High

This guide includes recovery, reset, or erase actions that can remove saved data. Do not continue until backups and account credentials are confirmed.

Check these first

Do not assume the cause from the symptom alone. First determine whether the issue affects the whole phone, one app, one connection, or one accessory. Work from low-impact checks toward recovery or repair.

  • Confirm that both the device and carrier support eSIM
  • Connect to stable Wi-Fi
  • Check whether the QR code is single-use or already installed on the old phone

Critical diagnosis for this symptom

QR再利用と回線発行状態を確認

Check QR reuse limits, EID/IMEI, Wi-Fi, and the line state on the old phone.

Before changing settings

Do not delete an eSIM until a reissue path is confirmed.

When to escalate

Escalate with device, OS, error, and reproduction details when the issue persists after restart, controlled comparison, and update checks, or when physical damage, abnormal heat, account suspension, or carrier provisioning is suspected.

Safe step-by-step process

Complete one step at a time and test after each change. Do not change several settings at once.

  1. 1
    Update the OS, set date and time automatically, and connect to reliable Wi-Fi.
  2. 2
    Open Add eSIM in SIM or Mobile settings and use the carrier QR code or official carrier app.
  3. 3
    Display the QR code clearly on another screen, reduce glare, and clean the camera. Use a manual code only through official instructions.
  4. 4
    Before removing the old line, confirm whether the carrier supports eSIM quick transfer or requires a new activation.
  5. 5
    Before deleting an installed eSIM, verify that it can be downloaded again and whether identity checks or fees apply.
  6. 6
    If activation remains pending, record the EID, IMEI, and exact error, then contact the carrier.

Actions to avoid

  • Deleting the old eSIM before the new phone is confirmed working
  • Sharing an activation QR code or code with another person
  • Repeatedly deleting and adding the same eSIM profile

Prevent the issue from returning

  • Review the carrier transfer process and support hours before changing phones
  • Keep EID, IMEI, and account access available in a secure place
If the problem remains

Record the conditions that reproduce the issue: phone model, OS version, time, exact error, recent changes, and steps already tried. This gives the manufacturer, carrier, or app developer useful diagnostic information.

Frequently asked questions

What if my screen looks different?

Menu names and locations vary by OS, manufacturer, model, region, and software version. Use the Settings search field and check the manufacturer guide for your exact model.

When should I stop troubleshooting?

Stop as soon as the issue is fixed. Change one setting at a time so you can identify what solved the problem and reverse it if necessary.

What should I do if the issue remains?

Record how to reproduce it, verify your backup, and contact the manufacturer, carrier, or app provider most closely related to the problem.

This is general operational guidance. Labels and effects vary by OS, model, carrier, region, and device-management policy. Confirm official instructions and backups before any erase, account, recovery, or repair action.
