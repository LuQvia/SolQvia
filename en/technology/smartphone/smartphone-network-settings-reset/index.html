How to Reset Network Settings on a Smartphone

Understand what a network-settings reset removes and how to prepare before using it for Wi-Fi, Bluetooth or mobile-data problems.

Data impact: review first

Saved settings or some data may be affected. Confirm the exact scope before continuing.

Before you start

Do not begin with a broad reset. Check the following first so you can isolate the problem and avoid unnecessary data loss or reconfiguration.

  • Know the passwords for Wi-Fi networks you need
  • Know how to pair important Bluetooth accessories again
  • Record VPN, APN, dual-SIM and related settings

Understand the process

Safe step-by-step procedure

Work from the top and stop as soon as the issue is resolved. Read every confirmation screen before moving to a higher-impact action.

  1. 1
    Try lower-impact steps first, including restart, Airplane Mode and forgetting only the affected Wi-Fi network.
  2. 2
    On iPhone, go to Settings > General > Transfer or Reset iPhone > Reset > Reset Network Settings; wording can vary by OS version.
  3. 3
    On Android, use Settings > System > Reset options and choose the Wi-Fi, mobile and Bluetooth reset; the path varies by manufacturer.
  4. 4
    Afterward, reconnect Wi-Fi and verify Bluetooth devices, VPN and cellular service one at a time.
  5. 5
    For managed phones or custom APN configurations, consult the administrator or carrier first.

Actions to avoid

  • Factory-resetting the whole phone for a network-only issue
  • Resetting without knowing required Wi-Fi passwords
  • Confusing an eSIM deletion with a network-settings reset

Prevention and preparation

  • Record important network settings securely
  • Reconnect one item at a time to verify the result
If the issue remains

Record whether the symptom occurs across apps, networks or accessories. Give official support the model, OS version, time, error text and steps already attempted.

Frequently asked questions

What if my screen looks different?

Labels and locations vary by OS, manufacturer, model and region. Use Settings search and check the manufacturer’s model-specific instructions.

What if I am unsure partway through?

Go back and confirm that no action has been committed. Do not proceed through an erase or account-change warning until you understand it.

What if the problem remains?

Test another connection or accessory where relevant, verify backups, then contact the manufacturer or carrier through official support.

This is general operational guidance. Screens and impact vary by OS, model, carrier and device management. Verify official instructions and backups before erasing data, changing accounts or arranging repair.
