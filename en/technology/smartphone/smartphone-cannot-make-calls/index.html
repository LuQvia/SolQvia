Phone Cannot Make Calls: Network, SIM, and Account Checks

Troubleshoot outgoing calls that fail, disconnect immediately, or show emergency calls only by checking coverage, SIM selection, restrictions, and carrier status.

Data impact：Medium

Changing settings, removing an app, or reconnecting a service can affect stored settings or unsynced data. Confirm the impact before continuing.

Check these first

Do not assume the cause from the symptom alone. First determine whether the issue affects the whole phone, one app, one connection, or one accessory. Work from low-impact checks toward recovery or repair.

  • Try more than one valid phone number
  • Look for No service, Emergency calls only, or Airplane mode indicators
  • Check carrier billing, suspension, roaming, and plan conditions

Critical diagnosis for this symptom

発信だけか回線全体かを確認

Determine whether only outgoing calls or the entire line is affected; check voice SIM, VoLTE, and account restrictions.

Before changing settings

Never use emergency numbers for testing.

When to escalate

Escalate with device, OS, error, and reproduction details when the issue persists after restart, controlled comparison, and update checks, or when physical damage, abnormal heat, account suspension, or carrier provisioning is suspected.

Narrow down the cause

Scope of the issueWhere to check next
Only one app is affectedCheck app permissions, updates, storage, cache, and reinstall options
The whole phone is affectedCheck restart, system updates, free storage, and system settings
Other devices have the same issueCheck the network, service, charger, or accessory
The issue began after a drop or liquid exposureAvoid repeated power or charging attempts and contact official support

Safe step-by-step process

Complete one step at a time and test after each change. Do not change several settings at once.

  1. 1
    Turn Airplane mode on for about ten seconds, then turn it off to register with the network again.
  2. 2
    Restart the phone and confirm the correct SIM or eSIM is active and selected for outgoing calls.
  3. 3
    Review caller-ID, call barring, fixed dialing, and call-blocking apps that might prevent outgoing calls.
  4. 4
    Move to another location and check the carrier outage and coverage information.
  5. 5
    Install carrier settings, network configuration, and OS updates. Reseat a physical SIM only as instructed by the carrier.
  6. 6
    If calls fail to several numbers in several locations, ask the carrier to verify account provisioning before requesting device diagnostics.

Actions to avoid

  • Calling emergency services merely to test the phone
  • Guessing a SIM PIN or PUK repeatedly
  • Factory-resetting before the carrier verifies the line

Prevent the issue from returning

  • Maintain access to the carrier app or account portal
  • Keep an alternative way to contact critical people and services
If the problem remains

Record the conditions that reproduce the issue: phone model, OS version, time, exact error, recent changes, and steps already tried. This gives the manufacturer, carrier, or app developer useful diagnostic information.

Frequently asked questions

What if my screen looks different?

Menu names and locations vary by OS, manufacturer, model, region, and software version. Use the Settings search field and check the manufacturer guide for your exact model.

When should I stop troubleshooting?

Stop as soon as the issue is fixed. Change one setting at a time so you can identify what solved the problem and reverse it if necessary.

What should I do if the issue remains?

Record how to reproduce it, verify your backup, and contact the manufacturer, carrier, or app provider most closely related to the problem.

This is general operational guidance. Labels and effects vary by OS, model, carrier, region, and device-management policy. Confirm official instructions and backups before any erase, account, recovery, or repair action.
