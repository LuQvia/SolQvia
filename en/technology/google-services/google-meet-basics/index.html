Google Meet Basics: Create, Join, Present, and Record Meetings

Create, join, present, and manage Google Meet sessions, including access, recording, and post-meeting controls.

Published: 2026-07-22Last updated: 2026-07-22
In this guide
  1. Overview
  2. Core capabilities
  3. Getting started
  4. Practical use cases
  5. Risks and checks

Overview

Google Meet is Google’s video-conferencing service. Participant limits, recording, noise cancellation, and administration vary by account and plan.

For adoption, define the target workflow, human review responsibility, and data rules before focusing on feature volume.

Core capabilities

  • Meetings can be created from Calendar or Gmail
  • Camera, audio, screen, and browser tabs can be shared
  • Hosts can manage admission, chat, and presenting
  • Some plans include recording, transcription, and related features
Key point

Feature names and usage limits change. This guide emphasizes durable selection criteria and operating practices.

Getting started

  1. Define the purpose, participants, and materials.
  2. Create the meeting from Calendar and review access.
  3. Check audio, camera, display name, and background before joining.
  4. Share only the intended window or tab.
  5. Afterward, review storage and sharing of recordings, chat, and files.

Practical use cases

Treat outputs as drafts, options, structured notes, or comparison support rather than automatically approved final work.

  • running internal meetings and external calls
  • conducting remote interviews and training
  • explaining procedures through screen sharing
  • retaining meeting records where permitted

Risks and checks

  • Review link forwarding and external admission settings
  • Inform participants about recording or transcription
  • Avoid exposing notifications or confidential tabs during sharing
  • Prepare an alternative contact method for connection failure
Key point

High-impact legal, medical, financial, contractual, and safety decisions require primary sources or qualified professional review.

Frequently asked questions

What should I try first?

Start with a small, reviewable task such as “running internal meetings and external calls” so a person can verify the result.

Can I use it for free?

Free access and trial conditions can change, so check the official pricing page when you sign up.

Can I enter confidential information?

Do not paste personal, customer, or unpublished information without checking the plan’s data terms and your organization’s policy. Anonymize data when possible.

This article provides general information. Check official sources for current specifications, prices, rules, and opening hours.
