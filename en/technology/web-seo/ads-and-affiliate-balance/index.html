How to Balance Display Ads and Affiliate Revenue

Use ads on informational pages and protect affiliate calls to action on comparison and high-intent pages.

Published: 2026-07-22Last updated: 2026-07-22
In this guide
  1. Why revenue sources compete
  2. A page-type strategy
  3. Metrics to track
  4. Run controlled tests

Why revenue sources compete

A reader who clicks a display ad may never reach an affiliate link or your own service. A small ad payment can replace a much higher-value conversion opportunity.

Do not use the same ad density everywhere. Segment pages by search intent and business role.

A page-type strategy

  • Definitions and general how-to guides: often suitable for display ads.
  • Comparisons, rankings, and individual reviews: protect affiliate calls to action.
  • Free calculators: keep ads away from inputs and results.
  • Your own service pages: generally avoid third-party ads.
  • High-traffic pages without a relevant offer: monetize otherwise unmonetized views with ads.

Metrics to track

  • Ad revenue by page
  • Affiliate-link click-through rate
  • Conversion and approval rate
  • Total revenue per 1,000 sessions
  • Engagement and exits
  • Core Web Vitals

Run controlled tests

  1. Save a pre-launch baseline, ideally covering several weeks.
  2. Add ads to only a subset of informational pages.
  3. Compare total revenue, not display-ad revenue alone.
  4. Test ads near calls to action, interstitials, and sticky units separately.
  5. Roll back changes that materially damage speed, trust, or conversion.
Key point

The optimization target is total value without undermining the page's purpose—not the highest possible page RPM.
