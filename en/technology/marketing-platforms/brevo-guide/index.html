Brevo Pricing and Features Guide: Choose by Send Volume

Evaluate Brevo by daily or monthly sends, stored contacts, automation, SMS, and WhatsApp.

How the product is positioned

Evaluate Brevo by daily or monthly sends, stored contacts, automation, SMS, and WhatsApp.

Unlike contact-centric pricing, Brevo requires review of send volume and add-on combinations.

How to read the pricing table

  • Daily or monthly email allowance
  • Stored-contact capacity
  • Automation contact limits
  • Brand removal and landing pages
  • SMS, WhatsApp, and transactional sends
Key point

Compare eligible records, sends, users, branding, AI, add-ons, and overage charges under the same assumptions rather than headline price alone.

What to test in the free tier or trial

  1. Calculate normal and peak monthly sends
  2. Separate campaigns and transactional mail
  3. Check automation contact volume
  4. Test sending-domain authentication
  5. Verify overage and credit behavior

Best-fit organizations and cautions

  • Whether the free daily cap fits operations
  • Whether stored contacts and send billing are distinguished
  • Whether country-specific SMS pricing is checked
  • Whether a dedicated IP is actually needed

Pre-contract checklist

  • Verified billable contacts, sends, and channels
  • Verified renewal pricing after annual discounts
  • Tested permissions and approval workflow
  • Tested migration of CSV, API data, and attachments
  • Verified post-cancellation access, export, and deletion terms
Key point

Pricing, free tiers, AI features, send limits, and integrations change. Verify the official pricing page and terms immediately before purchase.

Frequently asked questions

Does a small organization need Brevo Pricing and Features Guide?

Decide by data sensitivity, outage impact, and exposure rather than company size. Start with Daily or monthly email allowance, assign an owner, and set a review cycle.

Which baseline metrics should be recorded first?

Record volume, labor, failure rate, recovery time, and cost before implementation. Use the same definitions after rollout to measure benefit and side effects.

Is this a one-time setup?

No. Permissions, pricing, product behavior, threats, regulations, and team size change, so monthly or quarterly review is required.
