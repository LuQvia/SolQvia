Kit Pricing and Features Guide for Creator Newsletters

Evaluate Kit Newsletter, Creator, and Creator Pro by subscribers, automation, commerce, and recommendations.

  2. How to read the pricing table

How the product is positioned

Evaluate Kit Newsletter, Creator, and Creator Pro by subscribers, automation, commerce, and recommendations.

The free Newsletter plan includes product-specific recommendation conditions, so review the monetization model.

How to read the pricing table

  • Definition of active subscribers
  • Visual Automation limits
  • Broadcasts and landing pages
  • Digital products and paid newsletters
  • Recommendation and referral-revenue terms
Key point

Compare eligible records, sends, users, branding, AI, add-ons, and overage charges under the same assumptions rather than headline price alone.

What to test in the free tier or trial

  1. Test the free tier below 1,000 subscribers
  2. Create forms and a tagging model
  3. Build a welcome sequence
  4. Test product or paid-subscription checkout
  5. Verify subscriber export

Best-fit organizations and cautions

  • Whether it fits complex business permissions
  • Whether recommendation-slot conditions are understood
  • Whether tiered subscriber growth is modeled
  • Whether platform and payment fees are separated

Pre-contract checklist

  • Verified billable contacts, sends, and channels
  • Verified renewal pricing after annual discounts
  • Tested permissions and approval workflow
  • Tested migration of CSV, API data, and attachments
  • Verified post-cancellation access, export, and deletion terms
Key point

Pricing, free tiers, AI features, send limits, and integrations change. Verify the official pricing page and terms immediately before purchase.

Frequently asked questions

Does a small organization need Kit Pricing and Features Guide for Creator Newsletters?

Decide by data sensitivity, outage impact, and exposure rather than company size. Start with Definition of active subscribers, assign an owner, and set a review cycle.

Which baseline metrics should be recorded first?

Record volume, labor, failure rate, recovery time, and cost before implementation. Use the same definitions after rollout to measure benefit and side effects.

Is this a one-time setup?

No. Permissions, pricing, product behavior, threats, regulations, and team size change, so monthly or quarterly review is required.
