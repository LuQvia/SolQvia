Ahrefs Pricing and Features Guide: Projects, Credits, and AI Tracking

Evaluate Ahrefs Starter, Lite, Standard, Advanced, and Enterprise by projects, crawl credits, history, and users.

How the product is positioned

Evaluate Ahrefs Starter, Lite, Standard, Advanced, and Enterprise by projects, crawl credits, history, and users.

Plans differ in credits, history, tracked keywords, and additional-user rules, so quantify usage frequency.

How to read the pricing table

  • Projects and verified ownership
  • Crawl credits
  • Tracked keywords and prompts
  • Historical-data duration
  • Users, additional users, and API
Key point

Compare eligible records, sends, users, branding, AI, add-ons, and overage charges under the same assumptions rather than headline price alone.

What to test in the free tier or trial

  1. Verify ownership of your site
  2. Test Site Audit and Rank Tracker
  3. Run competitor and backlink research
  4. Measure one month of credit consumption
  5. Verify exports and sharing

Best-fit organizations and cautions

  • Whether Starter exposes required data
  • Whether credit-consuming actions are understood
  • Whether extra-user pricing is included
  • Whether AI tracking is separately priced

Pre-contract checklist

  • Verified billable contacts, sends, and channels
  • Verified renewal pricing after annual discounts
  • Tested permissions and approval workflow
  • Tested migration of CSV, API data, and attachments
  • Verified post-cancellation access, export, and deletion terms
Key point

Pricing, free tiers, AI features, send limits, and integrations change. Verify the official pricing page and terms immediately before purchase.

Frequently asked questions

Does a small organization need Ahrefs Pricing and Features Guide?

Decide by data sensitivity, outage impact, and exposure rather than company size. Start with Projects and verified ownership, assign an owner, and set a review cycle.

Which baseline metrics should be recorded first?

Record volume, labor, failure rate, recovery time, and cost before implementation. Use the same definitions after rollout to measure benefit and side effects.

Is this a one-time setup?

No. Permissions, pricing, product behavior, threats, regulations, and team size change, so monthly or quarterly review is required.

This article provides general information. Check official sources for current specifications, prices, rules, and opening hours.
