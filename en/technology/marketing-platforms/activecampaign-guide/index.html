ActiveCampaign Pricing and Features Guide: Automation and Contact Billing

Evaluate ActiveCampaign Starter, Plus, Pro, and Enterprise by automation, segmentation, sends, and channel add-ons.

Published: 2026-07-22Last updated: 2026-07-22
In this guide
  1. How the product is positioned
  2. How to read the pricing table
  3. What to test in the free tier or trial
  4. Best-fit organizations and cautions
  5. Pre-contract checklist

How the product is positioned

Evaluate ActiveCampaign Starter, Plus, Pro, and Enterprise by automation, segmentation, sends, and channel add-ons.

Lower tiers can limit automation actions and users, so count workflow complexity before pricing.

How to read the pricing table

  • Contacts and send multipliers
  • Automation action limits
  • Segmentation and conditional content
  • Users and permissions
  • SMS, WhatsApp, CRM, and AI add-ons
Key point

Compare eligible records, sends, users, branding, AI, add-ons, and overage charges under the same assumptions rather than headline price alone.

What to test in the free tier or trial

  1. Diagram a real automation
  2. Count required actions
  3. Test branches with representative contacts
  4. Verify ecommerce and CRM integrations
  5. Test pause, restart, and export

Best-fit organizations and cautions

  • Whether Starter action limits are sufficient
  • Whether one seat is operationally viable
  • Whether channel add-ons are included in cost
  • Whether attribution and predictive features are needed

Pre-contract checklist

  • Verified billable contacts, sends, and channels
  • Verified renewal pricing after annual discounts
  • Tested permissions and approval workflow
  • Tested migration of CSV, API data, and attachments
  • Verified post-cancellation access, export, and deletion terms
Key point

Pricing, free tiers, AI features, send limits, and integrations change. Verify the official pricing page and terms immediately before purchase.

Frequently asked questions

Does a small organization need ActiveCampaign Pricing and Features Guide?

Decide by data sensitivity, outage impact, and exposure rather than company size. Start with Contacts and send multipliers, assign an owner, and set a review cycle.

Which baseline metrics should be recorded first?

Record volume, labor, failure rate, recovery time, and cost before implementation. Use the same definitions after rollout to measure benefit and side effects.

Is this a one-time setup?

No. Permissions, pricing, product behavior, threats, regulations, and team size change, so monthly or quarterly review is required.

This article provides general information. Check official sources for current specifications, prices, rules, and opening hours.
