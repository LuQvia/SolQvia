Mailchimp vs Brevo: Contact-Based and Send-Volume Pricing

Compare Mailchimp and Brevo by billing unit, automation, transactional email, SMS, and contact management.

Published: 2026-07-22Last updated: 2026-07-22
In this guide
  1. How to frame the decision
  2. Compare under equal assumptions
  3. When Mailchimp fits better
  4. When Brevo fits better
  5. Final evaluation sequence

How to frame the decision

Compare Mailchimp and Brevo by billing unit, automation, transactional email, SMS, and contact management.

The better pricing model changes between large stored lists with low frequency and smaller lists with frequent sending.

Compare under equal assumptions

  • Billable contacts and send volume
  • Daily and monthly free limits
  • Automation volume and branching
  • Transactional email and SMS
  • Users, permissions, and branding

When Mailchimp fits better

  • Want integrated templates, ecommerce, and audience features
  • Can forecast contact count and send multipliers
  • Need Mailchimp-specific integrations

When Brevo fits better

  • Store many contacts and prefer send-volume pricing
  • Need transactional email, SMS, or WhatsApp together
  • Want to begin with a daily free allowance

Final evaluation sequence

  1. Price both options for the same volume, users, and contract term
  2. Reproduce the same data, campaign, measurement, and approval workflow
  3. Test limits, failures, cancellation, and offboarding
  4. Run real CSV and API exports
  5. Decide using three-year total cost and migration difficulty
Key point

Pricing and features can change after publication. Verify official pricing, terms, security documents, and trial results before purchase.

Frequently asked questions

Does a small organization need Mailchimp versus Brevo?

Decide by data sensitivity, outage impact, and exposure rather than company size. Start with testing the same data in both products, assign an owner, and set a review cycle.

Which baseline metrics should be recorded first?

Record volume, labor, failure rate, recovery time, and cost before implementation. Use the same definitions after rollout to measure benefit and side effects.

Is this a one-time setup?

No. Permissions, pricing, product behavior, threats, regulations, and team size change, so monthly or quarterly review is required.
