Semrush Pricing and Features Guide: SEO Classic and AI Visibility

Evaluate Semrush SEO Classic, AI Visibility, Social, and add-ons by projects, keywords, users, and toolkits.

How the product is positioned

Evaluate Semrush SEO Classic, AI Visibility, Social, and add-ons by projects, keywords, users, and toolkits.

Costs can expand across toolkits and App Center products, so separate required research and monitoring jobs first.

How to read the pricing table

  • Monitored sites and projects
  • Tracked keywords and locations
  • History, competitor, and content features
  • AI-search visibility tracking volume
  • Additional users, API, and apps
Key point

Compare eligible records, sends, users, branding, AI, add-ons, and overage charges under the same assumptions rather than headline price alone.

What to test in the free tier or trial

  1. Limit target sites and keywords
  2. Test Site Audit and Position Tracking
  3. Review competitor-research output
  4. Price only required toolkits
  5. Test CSV, API, and report sharing

Best-fit organizations and cautions

  • Whether project limits are sufficient
  • Whether AI features require separate subscriptions
  • Whether extra-user cost is included
  • Whether data limits match workflow frequency

Pre-contract checklist

  • Verified billable contacts, sends, and channels
  • Verified renewal pricing after annual discounts
  • Tested permissions and approval workflow
  • Tested migration of CSV, API data, and attachments
  • Verified post-cancellation access, export, and deletion terms
Key point

Pricing, free tiers, AI features, send limits, and integrations change. Verify the official pricing page and terms immediately before purchase.

Frequently asked questions

Does a small organization need Semrush Pricing and Features Guide?

Decide by data sensitivity, outage impact, and exposure rather than company size. Start with Monitored sites and projects, assign an owner, and set a review cycle.

Which baseline metrics should be recorded first?

Record volume, labor, failure rate, recovery time, and cost before implementation. Use the same definitions after rollout to measure benefit and side effects.

Is this a one-time setup?

No. Permissions, pricing, product behavior, threats, regulations, and team size change, so monthly or quarterly review is required.
