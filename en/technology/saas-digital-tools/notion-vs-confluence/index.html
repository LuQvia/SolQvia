Notion vs Confluence for Wikis, Documents, and Project Knowledge

Compare Notion and Confluence by editing, databases, search, permissions, version history, Jira integration, AI, and migration.

Published: 2026-07-22Last updated: 2026-07-22
In this guide
  1. Decision summary
  2. Compare under equal conditions
  3. When Notion fits better
  4. When Confluence fits better
  5. Final pilot sequence

Decision summary

Compare Notion and Confluence by editing, databases, search, permissions, version history, Jira integration, AI, and migration.

The choice differs between a flexible documents-and-databases workspace and a structured wiki closely connected to the Atlassian ecosystem.

Compare under equal conditions

  • Page hierarchy, databases, and template flexibility
  • Search, labels, links, update notices, and ownership
  • Permissions by space, team, guest, and page
  • Version history, approval, audit, retention, and export
  • Connections to Jira, Slack, Drive, and existing tools

When Notion fits better

  • Combining documents and lightweight databases
  • Flexible page creation for smaller teams
  • Unifying projects, wiki content, and meeting notes
  • Prioritizing simple design and usability

When Confluence fits better

  • Connecting documentation to Jira-centered development and IT
  • Running a large wiki organized by spaces
  • Using detailed Atlassian administration and access
  • Maintaining substantial existing Confluence content and macros

Final pilot sequence

  1. Price both options for the same users and contract term
  2. Reproduce the same workflow and dataset
  3. Test administration, sharing, audit, and offboarding
  4. Run real import and export tests
  5. Choose based on three-year cost and migration difficulty
Key point

Pricing and features change frequently. Verify official pricing, terms, security documentation, and the trial environment before signing.

Frequently asked questions

Can Notion versus Confluence be tested for free?

Free tiers and trials change by product and date. Check the official pricing page for included features, limits, and what happens after the trial.

What should be verified before adoption?

Define the target process, users, data, approval responsibility, total cost, and export path before comparing feature lists.

How long should a small pilot run?

A two-to-four-week pilot on one representative workflow is usually enough to measure quality, labor, exceptions, and adoption.
