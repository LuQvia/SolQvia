Ransomware Readiness: Recoverable Backups and Response Planning

Prepare for ransomware with isolated backups, restoration tests, separated privileges, and response procedures.

Published: 2026-07-22Last updated: 2026-07-22
In this guide
  1. Purpose of this control
  2. Management elements to prioritize
  3. Implementation sequence
  4. What to verify
  5. Ongoing operations checklist

Purpose of this control

Prepare for ransomware with isolated backups, restoration tests, separated privileges, and response procedures.

Verify that backups are isolated from attackers and can be restored within the required time, not merely that copies exist.

Management elements to prioritize

  • Multiple copies based on the 3-2-1 principle
  • Backups protected from deletion and encryption
  • Recovery time and restoration sequence
  • Separated administrative privileges
  • Decision path for ransom demands
Key point

Evaluate ownership, covered assets, evidence, exceptions, and recovery rather than product names alone.

Implementation sequence

  1. Classify critical data
  2. Define backup destinations and retention
  3. Separate backup and production administrators
  4. Run quarterly restoration exercises
  5. Prepare supplier, insurer, and legal contact procedures

What to verify

  • Whether backup credentials differ from production
  • Whether full-loss recovery time has been measured
  • Whether SaaS data is included
  • Whether post-restore safety validation is defined

Ongoing operations checklist

  • Named an owner and backup owner
  • Can update the asset and data inventory
  • Retain logs and change history
  • Tested stop, isolation, and recovery procedures
  • Scheduled monthly or quarterly review
Key point

Security controls do not guarantee that incidents will never occur. Design layered defenses to reduce impact and accelerate detection and recovery.

Frequently asked questions

Does a small organization need Ransomware Readiness?

Decide by data sensitivity, outage impact, and exposure rather than company size. Start with Multiple copies based on the 3-2-1 principle, assign an owner, and set a review cycle.

Which baseline metrics should be recorded first?

Record volume, labor, failure rate, recovery time, and cost before implementation. Use the same definitions after rollout to measure benefit and side effects.

Is this a one-time setup?

No. Permissions, pricing, product behavior, threats, regulations, and team size change, so monthly or quarterly review is required.
