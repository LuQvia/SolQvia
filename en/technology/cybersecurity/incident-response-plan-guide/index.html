Incident Response Plan: Contacts, Containment, Recovery, and Evidence

Define decision makers, contacts, containment, evidence, recovery, and lessons before an incident.

Published: 2026-07-22Last updated: 2026-07-22

  1. Purpose of this control
  2. Management elements to prioritize
  3. Implementation sequence
  4. What to verify
  5. Ongoing operations checklist

Purpose of this control

Define decision makers, contacts, containment, evidence, recovery, and lessons before an incident.

Exercise the plan with real contacts and permissions, integrating response into normal risk management.

Management elements to prioritize

  • Severity and escalation criteria
  • Roles for leadership, IT, legal, and communications
  • Containment for devices, identities, and networks
  • Preservation of logs, devices, and timelines
  • Recovery decisions and external notification
Key point

Evaluate ownership, covered assets, evidence, exceptions, and recovery rather than product names alone.

Implementation sequence

  1. Choose three major incident scenarios
  2. Create contact trees and alternate channels
  3. Document decision authority
  4. Record gaps during tabletop exercises
  5. Assign owners and deadlines for improvements

What to verify

  • Whether after-hours contacts work
  • Whether response works if an administrator is compromised
  • Whether log retention is sufficient
  • Whether reinfection checks follow recovery

Ongoing operations checklist

  • Named an owner and backup owner
  • Can update the asset and data inventory
  • Retain logs and change history
  • Tested stop, isolation, and recovery procedures
  • Scheduled monthly or quarterly review
Key point

Security controls do not guarantee that incidents will never occur. Design layered defenses to reduce impact and accelerate detection and recovery.

Frequently asked questions

Does a small organization need Incident Response Plan?

Decide by data sensitivity, outage impact, and exposure rather than company size. Start with Severity and escalation criteria, assign an owner, and set a review cycle.

Which baseline metrics should be recorded first?

Record volume, labor, failure rate, recovery time, and cost before implementation. Use the same definitions after rollout to measure benefit and side effects.

Is this a one-time setup?

No. Permissions, pricing, product behavior, threats, regulations, and team size change, so monthly or quarterly review is required.
