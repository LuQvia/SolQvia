Build an Expense Log Spreadsheet Template

Track expense date, vendor, category, payment method, receipt evidence, employee reimbursement, and review status.

Published: 2026-07-22Last updated: 2026-07-22
In this guide
  1. Purpose of the expense log
  2. Recommended columns
  3. Input workflow
  4. Summary controls

Purpose of the expense log

An expense log should support more than totals. It should show whether evidence exists, who paid, and whether reimbursement or review is complete.

Final accounting treatment depends on applicable policy and professional advice, but clean source data speeds up the monthly process.

Recommended columns

  • Expense ID, usage date, and payment date.
  • Vendor, description, and expense category.
  • Tax category, net amount, tax, and gross amount.
  • Payment method, employee payer, and reimbursement status.
  • Evidence URL, note, and reviewer.

Input workflow

  1. Register the record when the expense occurs.
  2. Link the receipt or invoice location.
  3. Choose category and tax treatment from controlled lists.
  4. Have a reviewer confirm the content and evidence.
  5. Update status after reimbursement or accounting transfer.

Summary controls

  • Totals by month and category.
  • Totals by location or department.
  • Counts of missing evidence, unapproved, and unreimbursed items.
  • Potential duplicates with the same date, amount, and vendor.
Key point

Use the expense ID in evidence filenames to make later retrieval easier.

This article provides general information. Check official sources for current specifications, prices, rules, and opening hours.
