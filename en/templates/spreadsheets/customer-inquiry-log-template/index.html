Prevent Missed Responses with a Customer Inquiry Log Template

Centralise channel, customer, request, owner, deadline, contact history, and completion status.

Published: 2026-07-22Last updated: 2026-07-22
In this guide
  1. Centralise follow-up
  2. Recommended columns
  3. Example statuses
  4. Operating controls

Centralise follow-up

Requests may arrive by phone, email, messaging, or form, but follow-up should be tracked in one register.

Limit personal data in the shared log to what is necessary for the response.

Recommended columns

  • Inquiry ID, received time, and channel.
  • Customer ID, name, and contact method.
  • Category, summary, and urgency.
  • Owner, first-response due date, and final due date.
  • Status, last contact time, and next action.

Example statuses

  • New, investigating, waiting for customer.
  • Waiting internally, in progress, resolved.
  • On hold, no action, duplicate.

Operating controls

  1. Issue an ID at intake.
  2. Always assign an owner and first-response deadline.
  3. Append concise chronological contact notes.
  4. Record the response and closure reason.
  5. Review overdue and long-held records weekly.
Key point

An inquiry without an owner is not controlled, even if it has been recorded.
