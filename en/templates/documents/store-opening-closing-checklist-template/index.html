Design a Store Opening and Closing Checklist Template

Arrange safety, equipment, cash, cleaning, bookings, and locking checks in time order with named performers and reviewers.

Published: 2026-07-22Last updated: 2026-07-22
In this guide
  1. Separate the checklist logically
  2. Opening examples
  3. Closing examples
  4. Recording method

Separate the checklist logically

Keep opening and closing workflows separate and group them by time and role. Distinguish daily, weekly, and monthly checks.

Write the item and expected normal condition instead of only saying check it.

Opening examples

  • Entrance, evacuation route, and equipment condition.
  • Till opening balance and device startup.
  • Booking, staffing, and customer-note review.
  • Temperature, lighting, audio, and cleanliness.
  • Consumables and change replenishment.

Closing examples

  • Sales and cash close.
  • Secure personal data and documents.
  • Stop equipment and check fire and water risks.
  • Waste, cleaning, and next-day preparation.
  • Windows, entrances, keys, and security system.

Recording method

  1. Provide a performer field for each item.
  2. Require a second check for critical controls.
  3. Include escalation contacts and thresholds.
  4. Record the reason and action for incomplete items.
  5. Review and simplify the checklist periodically.
Key point

Prioritise controls that prevent material incidents rather than maximising the number of boxes.

This article provides general information. Check official sources for current specifications, prices, rules, and opening hours.
