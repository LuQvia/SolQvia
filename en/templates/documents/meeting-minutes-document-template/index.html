Create Meeting Minutes That Capture Decisions and Owners

Record purpose, decisions, open issues, owners, deadlines, and next review instead of transcribing every comment.

Published: 2026-07-22Last updated: 2026-07-22
In this guide
  1. Purpose of minutes
  2. Template sections
  3. Recording workflow
  4. Avoid

Purpose of minutes

Meeting minutes are an execution record, not a full transcript. They should make post-meeting actions clear.

Preparing the agenda and required decisions in advance also makes the notes shorter and more useful.

Template sections

  • Meeting, date, attendees, and purpose.
  • Key discussion points by agenda item.
  • Decisions and rationale.
  • Open issues and required information.
  • Action, owner, and deadline.
  • Next meeting and review items.

Recording workflow

  1. Prioritise decisions and actions during the meeting.
  2. Read back owners and due dates before closing.
  3. Edit the notes promptly.
  4. Share them with a correction deadline.
  5. Move actions into the task tracker.

Avoid

  • Vague actions such as consider with no owner.
  • Mixing opinions with approved decisions.
  • Unnecessary word-for-word personal comments.
  • Actions without a deadline.
Key point

A decision, owner, and deadline are the minimum elements needed for execution.
