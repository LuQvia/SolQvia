Business Risk Register Template

What this template is for

A Markdown template for risk, cause, impact, likelihood, treatment, owner, deadline, and residual risk.

Download the Markdown template

The key is recording residual risk and the decision to accept it after treatment. For legal requirements and filing deadlines, verify current official guidance and obtain professional advice where appropriate.

Fields and checks to include

  • Risk event, cause, and impact
  • Likelihood, severity, and priority
  • Preventive, detective, and recovery controls
  • Owner, deadline, and residual risk
Key point

Prioritize ownership, deadlines, evidence, and approval state over adding more fields.

Operating workflow

  1. Download the template and assign an owner and storage location.
  2. Test with sample data before deleting fields that appear unnecessary.
  3. Adapt validation, choices, and approval states to your process.
  4. Pilot for one month and record missing or duplicate fields in revision history.

What reviewers should verify

  • Are required and optional fields distinguished?
  • Are date, amount, and status validations configured?
  • Are editor and viewer permissions separated?
  • Can revision date and reason be recorded?

Ongoing operating rules

  • Assign an owner and backup
  • Put close dates or review cycles on the calendar
  • Retain exceptions with reason and approval
  • Review fields and permissions monthly or quarterly
  • Check changes to laws, schemes, and official forms
Key point

This article explains general operating design. Verify tax, labor, and legal decisions with current official guidance and qualified advisers.

Frequently asked questions

Does a small business need Business Risk Register Template?

Yes when the process repeats or changes hands. A lightweight register that fixes recording residual risk and the decision to accept it after treatment can prevent omissions during busy periods.

Do we need dedicated software from the beginning?

No. Start with CSV, a spreadsheet, or a shared document to stabilize fields and approvals. Consider specialist software when volume, integrations, or access control grows.

How often should the process be reviewed?

Review daily controls daily, close activities monthly, and policies, permissions, and forms at least quarterly or whenever requirements change. Verify legal deadlines with current official guidance.
